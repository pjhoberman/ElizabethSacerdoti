April Real Estate Update

 

Good vs. Bad Inventory

Let’s face it- there is a LOT of inventory out there.  In some markets, it has broken records.  However, inventory is never a good metric to look at- especially in the last year as foreclosures and short sales have dominated the market.  

What this means for sellers: Great homes are great in any market.  However, you must absolutely shine when you go on the market.  While some of your competition may drive down prices in the neighborhood, to get top dollar you must show buyers VALUE in your home.

What this means for buyers:  Qualify what you want before you go out.  Keep re-evaluating what exactly you’re looking for.  There are thousands of three bed, two bath houses out there, so rather than look at 100 places, allow yourself to hone in on other things you want and don’t worry that you’re ‘missing’ inventory.  Maybe a garage is necessary for your camping and ski gear?  Maybe you really want to be within 5 minutes of the bus stop?  Does your dog need a yard with a fence?


The Market for the FSBO

Who are the For Sale by Owners?  How are their sales?  According to nation-wide sales data, 9% of sales were FSBO, showing a continued decrease over time.  14% of sales were FSBOs in 2003 and 2004, compared to just 9% in 2010.  Over ⅓ of FSBO sales were to family or friends.  The typical FSBO home sold for $140,000 compared to $199,300 for agent-assisted home sales.


Denver- Market in Review

March saw more supply coming to the market relative to the sales demand.  However, prices have been moving higher.  This trend may be the result of improved quality of homes being listed as we are still in a buyer’s market.  Median List price for a home in Denver rests at $225,000, for a condo, median list price is $178,000.


Most Expensive House in US- SOLD!

The most expensive one family hold ever sold in the United States was purchased last month for $100 million dollars.  Located in Los, Altos, CA, the 11 acre home has 5 bedrooms and 9 baths and resembles a french chateau.  Don’t worry though, it’s a steal at $3,921 per square foot!  It that doesn’t say, ‘ market rebound’, I don’t know what does.
