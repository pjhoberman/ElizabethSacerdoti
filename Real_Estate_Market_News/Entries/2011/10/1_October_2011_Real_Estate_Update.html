Where to Live?  The best value Denver ‘hoods

5280 Magazine annually publishes a great article about the best central Denver neighborhoods .  In fact, here’s a link to their last article.   5280 Magazine Article

I totally agree with their choices this year. I would further comment that they almost all have one thing in common.  They all border neighborhoods that historically have been very strong.  The reason for this trend is that over-speculation of these neighborhoods- the Highlands, Wash Park East, Cherry Creek- was unsustainable through the recession.  However, these peripheral areas have been strong investments for buyers in the last 18 months.  Long term, these investments will absolutely pay off as buyers benefit two fold- they also got incredible interest rates on neighborhoods that have no where to go but up.

What’s Happening with New Construction?

The United States Census Bureau listed Denver as #20 for new housing construction projects in 2010.  The study involved 366 metro areas and the total number of permitted and approved building projects.    Houston, Dallas and New York City rounded out the top three, while Colorado Springs ranked #42.  This is very positive news for Colorado as new construction tends to be a strong indicator of overall economic health.


What’s happening with Colorado foreclosures?

They are declining!  The second quarter showed a 30% reduction over the second quarter last year for new filings for foreclosures.  This means that some  the inventory that has been held by banks is slowly tapering off.  Over time, this will bring the market further into balance as we see declines in the massive amount of bank inventory that flooded the market in the last two years.


What to know about your credit report

Parking Tickets can now show up on your credit report!  Beware!  If you see an error somewhere on the report, discuss whether or not you should dispute the charge or simply pay it to make it disappear.  The timing on fighting credit report errors can be the difference between closing on your home or staying in your rental.  Only your lender can guide you on how this can affect you.
