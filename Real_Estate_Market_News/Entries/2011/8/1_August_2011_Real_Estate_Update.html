Denver is 4th for Oil and Gas Professionals

Rigzone.com reports that Denver/Boulder is the 4th among up and coming energy cities for oil and gas professionals.  The study asked to consider quality of life, weather, professional conditions and appeal.  Denver was the only city in the United States to make the list, Dubai, Calgary and Rio de Janeiro rounded out the top three.  Positive reports among major industries in our city will only reflect positive long term news for our housing market as well.


The Denver Market in Review

Home sales continue to exceed new inventory for both condos and single family homes, which has led prices slightly upward- more so for single family homes.  Days on market remains unchanged as well- hovering between 117-156 for homes and 145-200 for condos.  This also means that most homes on the market- around 46%-  experience a price drop before they sell.  As a result, competitive pricing and remaining ahead of the market are critical to selling quickly.  Nationally, home prices just announced a 4.5% fall, however, some neighborhoods, especially in urban Denver neighborhoods remain hot.   In fact, the number of homes placed under contract in May and June rose 23% over last year.
