Who’s Helping the US Housing Market?  The World

CNN ran an interesting article a few days ago citing growing international investment in US properties.  Because these sales have so dramatically helped places like South Florida, the senate is putting forth a bill that will actually grant US visas to investors who spend $500k or more on residential property. Last March, according to the National Association of Realtors, international buyers represented 8% of the total US sales- $82 billion in a 12 month period.  This figure represents a 24% increase from the 12 months prior.  Some markets, 25% of buyers are international. If the bill passes, we may well see a sharp rise in sales, especially in the major US markets.  


Union Station Update!

The Denver Post has just reported two different proposals for the 19 acre Union Station redevelopment, slated to open in spring of 2014.  The Downtown Denver Partnership recently hosted an event for realtors to see first hand what these changes will be.

Plan One: Union Station Neighborhood Co. - The train station will have a restaurant, a walk-thru food market, new waiting room amenities, an information kiosk and about 25,000 square feet of leasable office space.

Plan Two: Union Station Alliance - A boutique 130 room hotel, connected to the Oxford Hotel with additional restaurant and office space as well.

RTD will make a formal recommendation on one of these plans by the end of the month and the board will make a decision early next year.  Stay tuned for more info on the future of downtown Denver!
