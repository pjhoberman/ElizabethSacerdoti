April Real Estate Update

 

Dear Friends,

Spring fever has definitely hit with the arrival of warm beautiful weather.  This change in the air has seemed to bring a few new things back to the real estate market as well.  Inventory has been picking up considerably in the market and there is quite a bit of momentum building – homes that are well priced and show nicely are starting to sell and I’ve had several scenarios with multiple offer situations on homes that had sat for lengthy periods of time.  What this means is that buyers are starting to find more properties that may fit their needs, but are needing to move on them quickly.  Sellers are starting to regain confidence as well and this is beginning to reflect in many more showings and a lot more interest.   Many in the industry are predicting a strong start in the spring and summer months and renewed stability in the Denver market.

Landscaping: Thoughts

You don’t have to live in Denver long to notice that despite our love of the outdoors there are more than a few people out there who don’t have a green thumb.  Fair enough, but curb appeal is so critical to the sale of your home that you need to spend some time out there weeding your beds, planting a few annuals and maintaining your front yard.  This also extends to touching up any paint that has cracked on your trim and washing windows.  If you need any motivation, stop over at my house, I’ve been replacing the soffit of my own front porch for the last few weeks.  Its also a wonderful way to get to know your neighbors better and let them know you’ll be putting your home on the market soon.  People have strong reactions to the curb appeal of your home and a few well landscaped areas may well be what it takes to get your home sold quickly.

Spring Cleaning!

Recently, I’ve fielded a lot of questions about what projects make the most sense to get your home ready to sell.  One of the first things I like to encourage is to Clean Thoroughly- this is more important than almost anything else.  Even if you are an extremely tidy person, there are things you miss in your day to day cleaning routine that I recommend either attending to these items or bringing in an professional for a day ( about $250) to get you in show-ready condition.  Keep in mind your home will be in competition with professionally staged and vacant homes that are almost always in perfect condition.
