August Real Estate Update

 

Denver Neighborhood’s 6 Month Report


Overall, the good news this year is that generally speaking, Denver is clearly pulling out of the housing slump earlier than many other cities.  As were one of the first cities in the recession due to our higher foreclosure rates, we are seemingly going to be one of the first ones out.


So far this year we’ve seen more homes sold in most of the major Denver neighborhoods than this time last year.  The only neighborhoods that saw fewer home sales than last year were City Park South, Congress Park, Crestmoor, Harvard Park, Hilltop and Southern Hills.  Everywhere else saw large increases in homes sold- for example, Potter Highlands had 17 homes sold between January -June in 2009.  This year, they had 32.  Bonnie Brae had 37 sold in that time, this year they had 50.


The other metric I look at is the average price per square foot.  In total for Denver neighborhoods across the board, last year we saw an average of $263/sq. ft.  This year, its up to $265/sq/ ft.  Some neighborhoods did exceptionally well, including Whittier which rose from $200 to $239 per square foot and Crestmoor which rose from $219 to $265.  For a full report on your neighborhood, please see the image below.
























Tip for 1st time Buyers:


If you have generous parents that would be willing to co-sign on a loan, than you’re in luck~ if all you have is a good credit rating, but you’re worried that your finances won’t be enough to qualifiy for a good loan, than your parent’s may be able to help you out.  It’s certainly worth entertaining the thought as this is clearly one of the best times to  invest in real estate!


Re-Financing


I don’t make to make blanket statements about anything in real estate, but I feel compelled to strongly encourage everyone out there- if the loan on your house is more than about 6%, than go out and refinance your house.  It’s almost assuredly worth looking into.  Today, Land Title reports that 78% of loan applications are related to refinancing and with an average 30 year fixed loan hovering around 4.68% the month of July, you could save hundreds of dollars a month on your loan.
