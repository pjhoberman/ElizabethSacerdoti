May Real Estate Update

 

End of the Tax Credit: What Will Happen Next?

 

There has been a lot of speculation in my industry about whether or not the market will be affected now that the first time buyer tax credit has expired.  According to a Rismedia study, consumer confidence remains high in the real estate market with over 65% of those polled saying the end of the credit will not affect their decision to buy a home.  Even more important, this study found that 46% of people felt the real estate values in their neighborhood would increase in the next year.  With the possibility of increasing mortgage rates and unemployment still looming, truly only time will tell.  However, I’ve found the start of summer to be tremendously busy time for my office with many new listing and new buyers entering the market at rates we haven’t seen in several years.

 

Forbes Magazine: Talking Trash About Denver?


April 5th was a shocking day in the Denver real estate world.  Forbes Magazine published an unflattering report on the Denver real estate market only to be met with harsh criticism from a wide number of Denverites- from Mayor John Hickenlooper to the entire real estate community.  Essentially, the article relied extensively on information provided by Zillow which proved to be grossly inaccurate due to double counting in Zillow’s statistics of unsold homes.  As a result, Forbes has retracted their statement and said they will no longer rely on Zillow’s inventory data.  Take That!
