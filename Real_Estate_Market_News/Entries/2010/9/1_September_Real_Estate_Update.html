September Real Estate Update

 

Farewell, McMansion

The age of the McMansion is officially over.  For the first time in a long time, the average American home size is actually getting smaller.  Great news for the environment as less space also means fewer materials, less cost and a smaller overall footprint.  In light of the recession, more people either stayed in their existing home or made renovations that rendered them more functional.  As  result, people have begun to realize that perhaps they don’t really need 5,000 square feet.  I always make a point of encouraging buyers to see rooms in a variety of ways, so that as their needs change, changing the use of spaces may allow them to remain functional.  New construction homes that have 4-5 “reading nooks” with no real usable space are on their way out. The new median home size is 2,135 square feet in 2009 according to the census bureau and CNN Money.  This is a drop from 2,300 square feet earlier in the decade.  A possible explanation is a trend towards more multi-functioning space and less single purpose rooms, like the media room...(see below)


Market in Review

The news is mixed on real estate today. Statistics are all pointing in different directions and every article I read tells a different story.  The bottom line is that, YES, home prices have increased 3.6% over last year.  However home sales plummeted 27% after the tax credit expired.  In Denver, many neighborhoods saw an increase in price per square foot from 2009 (see last month’s update), but fewer home sales over a 12-month period.  On the front line of real estate, I can tell you that homes in perfect show condition and that are priced well are selling, sometimes with multiple offers. I recently sold a home after recieving 4 offers, 2 of which came in the same day after 7 weeks on the market.  Buyers are out there, but they have certainly developed a more skilled knowledge base about the industry and are extremely well informed.  Buyers are questioning the investment element of real estate in as much detail as they are thinking about the house itself.  Although it may mean a lean year for real estate, personally, I like to think that the media’s intense scrutiny on real estate will mean an increasingly informed public for many years to come.  That can only be better for the industry.

Buy Airconditioning, Seriously.

Appliances are a big deal.  Today, almost 90% of new homes have air conditioning.  63% of all existing homes having a cooling system.  Only a decade ago, this number was closer to 50%, which indicates that buyers want to see those upgrades in your house.  This trend is further exemplified by increases across the board from items ranging from garbage disposals to dishwashers.  Your kitchen has always been a great place to invest in your home, but every-day conveniences are a must.  Also note, the federal government rebate program has made many energy star appliances more affordable than ever!


Interest Rates- 40 year low!

It's hard to imagine they've dipped lower, but interest rates are lower now than at any point in the last 40 years. Buyers, you purchasing power has never been higher!  Homeowners, if you haven't refinanced in the last 3 years, you should!  Interest rates are hovering around 4.25%.
